General Information

What is a Heart Attack?

A heart attack occurs when the flow of blood to the heart is blocked. The blockage is most often a build-up of fat, cholesterol and other substances, which form a plaque in the arteries that feed the heart (coronary arteries).

What should I know about my heart?

The heart is the hardest working muscle in the human body. The heart is located almost in the centre of the chest. The adult human heart is about the size of a fist. The heart beats at an average rate of 80 times a minute.

Does stress affect your heart?

Stress can increase inflammation in your body, which in turn is linked to factors that can harm your heart, such as high blood pressure and lower “good” HDL cholesterol, Blaha says.

Symptoms

A heart attack or stroke may be the first sign of underlying disease. Symptoms of a heart attack include:

Pain or discomfort in the centre of the chest; and/or.

Pain or discomfort in the arms, the left shoulder, elbows, jaw, or back.

Numbness of the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body.

Difficulty seeing with one or both eyes.

Confusion, difficulty speaking or understanding speech.

Difficulty walking, dizziness and/or loss of balance or coordination.

Severe headache with no known cause; and/or.

Fainting or unconsciousness.

Prevention

Control your blood pressure

Eat a healthy diet.

Keep your cholesterol and triglyceride levels under control.

Stay at a healthy weight

Get regular exercise.

Limit alcohol

Manage stress

Manage diabetes

Make sure that you get enough sleep.

Don't smoke
